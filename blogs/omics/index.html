Multi-omics

Most pig economic traits are complex traits, which are under the regulation of complex interaction among multiple genes. Thus, understanding the genetic architecture of these traits would greatly benefit from approaches integrating multi-omics data, such as genomics, epgienomics, transcriptomics, proteomics and metabolomics, etc.. Here, we are developing novel experimental methods and computational methods to integrate multi-omics data to elucidate the genetic basis of important economic traits of pigs. We are also continuously expanding our germplasm resources to profile genetic variation and phenotypic variation both at the organism-trait level and the molecular-trait level. Our research highlights include the first chromosomal-scale haplotype-resolved assembly of a Chinese indigenous pig breed, integrating population genetics, transcriptomics and functional genomics to identify novel genes implicated in disease resistance and meat qulity, and the development of high-throughput methods for fine-mapping functional cis-regulatory SNPs associated with complex traits.
