AI-based deep learning

Genomic selection has been a hot spot in agricultural breeding. Comparing with traditional breeding strategy, it provides opportunities to improve genetic gains for many complex traits. Past research in genomic selection mainly focused on building statistical models that predict phenotypes by genotype data alone. It is well known that information flows from DNA to phenotype through multiple layers of regulatory networks and intermediate molecular phenotypes. Thus, we believe that artificial intelligence models that capture the architecture of information flow would greatly improve the phenotype prediction accuracy. Based on this rationale, we are building deep learning-based genomic selection models that make full use of the multi-omics information, such as data of genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, and metabolomics. By keeping the topology of network structure in the model, we also aim to fine-map causal genetic variants by tracing back functional projections.
