Synthetic Biology

Following reading and decoding of the information in animal genomes, by using synthetic biology approaches to modify or even build a genome, it will fundamentally transform how we grow food, what we eat and where we source materials and medicines. Based on the concept of “synthetic breeding”, we are dedicated to building a pig synthetic biology platform, to collect and aggregate the DNA sequences causing superior phenotypes into in the same pig line, which was named “SuperPig”. We are also establishing a system for evaluating the biological safety and performance of genetically engineered pigs. Apart from generating pigs for food, we are developing genetically-edited pigs to serve as medical models for human diseases. To develop pigs as organ donors for humans, we are exploiting gene editing to modify porcine hyperacute rejection factors and endogenous retroviral genes, as well as analyzing the molecular mechanism of chronic rejection.
