Genome Editing

As a simple and highly efficient genome editing tool, the CRISPR system has been quickly and widely adopted in the life sciences, basic medicine, and agricultural breeding. Our institute is committed to being a leader in the optimization and application of the CRISPR system in animals. We have developed the “C-CRISPR” technology, with which we can obtain, in the F0 generation, mice and monkeys with the complete knockout of single or multiple genes. We further modified the CRISPR system to allow for knockout of a whole chromosome. We recently developed a new generation of gene-editing off-target detection technology named “GOTI”, providing an important evaluation system for comparing the performance of different CRISPR editors. For example, we found that the single-base editing tool, BE3, will cause completely random off-target effects within the genome. By comparison, a high-fidelity single-base editing tool, YE1-BE3-FNLS, was established through bioengineering technology, which solved the off-target problems of single-base editing tools.
