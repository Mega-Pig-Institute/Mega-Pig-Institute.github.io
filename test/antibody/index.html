Humanized antibody

Pigs are similar to humans in terms of body size, physiology, organ development, and disease etiology. Compared to horses, cattle and other large animals, pigs have a great advantage in fecundity. Therefore, pigs hold the promise of functioning as a biologics factory to efficiently produce humanized antibodies. We are aiming at introducing human immunoglobulin genes to replace the pig counterparts in pig embryonic stem cells, thereby building an anti-infective drug production platform. This platform can not only reduce the cost of antibody production, but the obtained human immunoglobulin can also change the current situation of no medicines for some infectious diseases. We envision that the platform will also play an important role in responding to abrupt public safety issues.
